Privacy Policy for oneclick free vpn

Effective date: July 12, 2026

This Privacy Policy explains how oneclick free vpn ("we", "us", or "our") handles information in connection with the Android app oneclick free vpn (com.oneclickvpn.tv).

Privacy contact: m980528@gmail.com

What the app does

oneclick free vpn lets users start a VPN connection on Android TV, Google TV, phones, and tablets. When you choose to connect and approve the Android VPN permission prompt, device network traffic is routed through an encrypted OpenVPN tunnel to an independent public volunteer VPN server.

The app does not operate the volunteer VPN servers. It obtains public server information from the VPN Gate Academic Experiment Project. VPN Gate, its logging partner, and volunteer server operators are independent third parties. Server availability, quality, speed, location, and privacy practices can vary. Do not treat public volunteer VPN servers as a guarantee of anonymity, security, or access to any specific website or service.

Information the app developer does not collect

The app does not require an account and does not ask you to provide your name, email address, phone number, contacts, photos, camera, microphone, precise GPS location, or payment card details.

The app does not include advertising SDKs or analytics SDKs. The app developer does not operate a VPN traffic database and does not receive VPN Gate's connection or packet logs. This does not mean that no data is logged by VPN Gate or volunteer server operators; their stated logging is described below.

Information handled by the app

The app may handle the following information to provide its features:

  • VPN traffic and connection metadata: When the VPN is connected, device network traffic passes through the VPN tunnel to the selected public volunteer VPN server. VPN Gate states that its connection logs record the connection date and time; destination VPN server ID, IP address, and hostname; connect or disconnect action; the raw source IP address and hostname; VPN protocol; VPN client software name, version, and ID when available; packet and byte totals; communication errors; and destination HTTP/HTTPS hostnames, IP addresses, hostnames, and port numbers. VPN Gate also states that each volunteer server records TCP/IP packet headers for communications initiated by VPN users.
  • Public IP check: After connecting, the app may contact a public IP information service to display the current public IP address, country, and network information. That service receives normal request metadata such as IP address and time of request.
  • Server directory: The app downloads a public list of available VPN servers over HTTPS. Normal request metadata such as IP address and time of request may be visible to the server directory provider.
  • Google Play purchase status: If you buy or restore Pro, Google Play processes the transaction. The app receives purchase status needed to unlock Pro features. The app does not receive your full payment card number.
  • Local app data: The app stores settings on your device, such as whether the first VPN disclosure was accepted, whether Pro is unlocked, the last connected server label/country, and a cached server list. If you export an OVPN profile, the profile may be saved locally or to Downloads if you choose that action.

How information is used

The app developer uses information only to provide app functionality: starting and maintaining the VPN connection, showing connection status, checking whether Pro is unlocked, caching the server list for reliability, and exporting profiles when requested. VPN Gate states that it uses connection and packet logs for anti-abuse, investigation, and legally authorized disclosure purposes.

Sharing with third parties

The app necessarily interacts with third parties to provide its features:

  • VPN Gate, its central logging partner, and independent public volunteer VPN server operators receive VPN traffic and connection metadata while you are connected.
  • A public IP information service receives requests when the app checks public IP information.
  • A public server directory provider receives requests when the app refreshes the server list.
  • Google Play handles purchases, refunds, and purchase status for the optional Pro unlock.
  • If you export an OVPN profile and open it in another app, that other app receives the profile file you chose to share.

Data retention and deletion

We do not maintain a user account database for oneclick free vpn. Local app data remains on your device until you clear app data, uninstall the app, or delete exported profile files yourself. Google Play purchase records are handled by Google according to Google Play's policies.

VPN Gate states that its central VPN connection logs are kept for three months or longer. It states that packet headers stored by each volunteer VPN server are kept for at least two weeks and may later be compressed or deleted. VPN Gate and volunteer operators control those records, not the app developer, so the app developer cannot delete them. VPN Gate states that logs may be disclosed to police, prosecutors, lawyers, courts, or other recipients when authorized under applicable law. Public IP and server directory providers may have their own retention practices.

You can request privacy assistance or deletion guidance by contacting m980528@gmail.com.

Security

The app uses HTTPS for its app-controlled public web requests and an encrypted OpenVPN tunnel from the device to the VPN endpoint. This does not guarantee end-to-end encryption after traffic exits the VPN endpoint, prevent the logging described above, or make unencrypted HTTP traffic confidential from the endpoint operator. You should still prefer HTTPS websites and trusted services.

Children

oneclick free vpn is not designed for children. We do not knowingly collect personal information from children.
